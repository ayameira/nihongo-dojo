# Japanese Study Notes

## Current Focus
- The student is currently focusing on conditional forms (〜と, 〜ば, 〜たら, 〜なら) and mastering their nuances.
- We are practicing the ~について (~ni tsuite) construction to mean "about". For example, 詩について話す (shi ni tsuite hanasu) means "talk about poetry."
- Expanding vocabulary related to daily life, technology, and travel in Japan.

## Recent Corrections & Struggles
- **Transitive vs Intransitive verbs**: The student frequently mixes up pairs like 開ける (akeru - to open something) and 開く (aku - something opens). We need to reinforce the corresponding particles (を vs が).
- **Keigo (Honorifics)**: The student sometimes struggles to switch smoothly between Sonkeigo (respectful) and Kenjougo (humble) language during roleplay scenarios.
- **Te-form conjugations**: Occasional slip-ups when conjugating verbs ending in む (mu), ぶ (bu), ぬ (nu) into the te-form (should be 〜んで).

## Interests & Preferences
- **Hobbies**: The student loves discussing video games, software development, and modern Japanese pop culture.
- **Goals**: Wants to be able to seamlessly hold a conversation with native speakers during their upcoming trip to Tokyo and understand technical documentation in Japanese.
- **Conversation Style**: Enjoys casual, fast-paced conversations but appreciates being corrected immediately when making grammatical mistakes. Needs lots of examples.
